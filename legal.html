1. Terms of Service

These Terms govern your use of DevDome (the "Service"), a community platform for pitching game ideas, finding collaborators, and chatting with accepted teammates. By creating an account or otherwise using the Service, you agree to these Terms. If you don't agree, please don't use the Service.

2. Accounts & Eligibility

  • You need an account (email/password, or sign-in via Google or GitHub) to post, vote, request to join a squad, or chat.
  • You're responsible for keeping your login credentials secure. Activity under your account is treated as activity by you.
  • One account per person. Creating multiple accounts to evade a rate limit, a moderation action, or to manipulate votes is not allowed.
  • Your username must be your own to pick, but it may not impersonate another real person or organization, contain slurs or hate speech, or be deliberately confusing/offensive.

3. Acceptable Use

You agree not to use DevDome to:

  • Post or send anything illegal, threatening, harassing, hateful, sexually explicit, or that promotes violence or self-harm
  • Impersonate any person or entity, or misrepresent your affiliation with one
  • Post spam, scams, phishing links, or unsolicited advertising
  • Upload or transmit malware, or attempt to disrupt, overload, or gain unauthorized access to the Service or other users' accounts
  • Scrape, bulk-extract, or systematically harvest data from the Service beyond normal use
  • Circumvent rate limits, moderation actions, or access controls (including via scripts, bots, or multiple accounts)
  • Post content you don't have the right to share (e.g. someone else's copyrighted art, code, or writing, without permission)

Reasonable, good-faith disagreement, critique, or negotiation between collaborators (e.g. over project direction or credit) is not a violation of these Terms — this section is about harmful or abusive conduct, not normal creative friction.

4. Your Content & Ownership

  • You keep ownership of whatever you post — pitches, squad listings, chat messages, your profile info. DevDome doesn't claim ownership of it.
  • By posting, you give DevDome the limited right to store, display, and transmit that content as needed to operate the Service (for example, showing your pitch to other users, or delivering your chat messages to the person you're messaging). That's it — this isn't a license to use your content for anything else, like advertising or training other products.
  • You're solely responsible for what you post. DevDome doesn't pre-screen content before it's published.
  • If you post a game idea publicly, treat it as something other users will see. DevDome isn't a confidentiality or NDA service — if an idea needs to stay secret, don't pitch the secret part here.

5. Moderation & Enforcement

  • You can delete your own pitches and squad posts at any time from your profile or the listing itself.

  • Moderation decisions on a small, independently-run project like this are made by a person, not a formal review board — they're made in good faith, but there's no formal appeals process beyond contacting the site owner directly (see Section 16).

10. How Your Data Is Used

Your data is used only to operate the Service: authenticating you, displaying your posts and profile to other users, delivering your messages to your intended recipient, and enforcing rate limits / moderation. It is not used for advertising (DevDome currently shows no ads), and is not sold to third parties.

11. Who Else Sees Your Data

  • Other users can see your username, skills, pitches, squad posts, and votes — these are public by design. Chat messages are visible only to you and the other participant in that specific conversation.
  • Supabase (our database and authentication provider) stores all of the above on our behalf, under their own security and privacy practices.
  • GitHub Pages (or wherever this site is hosted) serves the website files themselves; it does not have access to your account data, which lives in Supabase, not in the static site.
  • Google / GitHub (only if you use their sign-in option) handle the authentication handshake per their own privacy policies — DevDome only receives the basic profile info needed to create your account.
  • We do not share your data with advertisers, data brokers, or any other third party for marketing purposes.
  • We may disclose data if legally required to do so (for example, in response to a valid court order), or to protect the safety of users in good faith.

12. Your Rights & Choices

  • Access: most of your data is already visible to you directly in the app (your profile, pitches, squad posts).
  • Correction: you can edit your username and skills at any time from your profile.
  • Deletion of content: you can delete your own pitches and squad posts at any time.
  • Deletion of account: to delete your entire account and associated data, contact the site owner (Section 16). Because this is a small project without automated self-service account deletion yet, this is currently handled manually — we'll aim to act on it within a reasonable time.
  • If you are in the EU/UK, you may have additional rights under GDPR (e.g. data portability, the right to object to processing). If you are a California resident, you may have rights under the CCPA. Contact us to exercise these rights — see Section 16.

13. Data Retention & Deletion

Your data is retained for as long as your account exists, so the Service can function (showing your past pitches, maintaining chat history with collaborators, etc.). If your account is deleted, associated personal data is removed from the live database; some content that other users depend on (for example, a chat thread) may be removed for both participants if your account is deleted, since the conversation requires both accounts to exist.

14. Age Requirement

DevDome is not directed at children. You must be at least 13 years old to use the Service (or the minimum age required in your country to consent to use of an online service without parental consent, if higher). If you believe a child has created an account in violation of this, contact us and we will remove it.

We may update these Terms and this Privacy Policy from time to time as the Service changes. We'll update the "Last updated" date above when we do. Continued use of the Service after a change means you accept the updated terms. For significant changes, we'll try to give more visible notice (e.g. an in-app banner) where practical.
